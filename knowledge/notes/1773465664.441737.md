# Summary

Google has officially named its new London headquarters **"Platform 37"** in tribute to a historic AI breakthrough from a decade ago. The name references the famous 37th move made by Google DeepMind's AlphaGo during its match against world champion Go player Lee Sedol—a move initially considered a mistake by experts but which proved to be a brilliant strategic decision that helped win the match.

## Key Points:

- **Opening**: Set to begin operations in summer 2026
- **Purpose**: Will serve as Google's AI operations center in Europe
- **Size**: 330 meters long, exceeding London's The Shard (310m)
- **Significance**: Google's first fully-owned and designed facility outside the U.S.
- **Design**: Created by architects Thomas Heatherwick and Bjarke Ingels
- **Development**: Project first announced in 2013, faced multiple delays
- **Public Access**: An "AI Exchange Center" will open by year-end, allowing visitors to explore AI technology

The facility represents Google's commitment to AI's future and reinforces London's position as a global hub for AI research and development.
