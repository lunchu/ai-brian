# Summary

The U.S. Commerce Department withdrew a proposed rule on AI chip exports on the 13th, without providing specific reasons.

**Key Points:**

- **Background**: The draft rule, submitted for inter-agency review in late February, was part of the Trump administration's effort to replace Biden-era policies

- **Biden vs. Trump approach**: 
  - Biden's framework: Exempted most U.S. allies, divided markets into three tiers, aimed to restrict China's access while maintaining U.S. AI leadership
  - Trump's proposed plan: Would require foreign governments to invest in U.S. data centers or provide security guarantees for exports exceeding 200,000 AI chips

- **Internal conflict**: The withdrawal likely reflects disagreements within the Trump administration on how to achieve global AI dominance and handle national security concerns

- **Commerce Department stance**: Called Biden's framework "burdensome, overly interventionist, and disastrous," though insiders say the new draft was similarly cumbersome and restrictive

- **Status**: New rules still under discussion, expected to differ significantly from previous framework
