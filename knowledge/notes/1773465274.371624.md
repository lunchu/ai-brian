# Summary

**U.S. Commerce Department Withdraws AI Chip Export Rule**

**Key Points:**

- **March 13**: U.S. Commerce Department withdrew a proposed rule regulating AI chip exports
- **No explanation given** for the withdrawal; department spokesperson declined to comment

**Background:**
- The draft rule was sent to other agencies for review in late February
- Seen as Trump administration rolling back Biden-era policies
- Biden's framework (Jan 2025) tiered global markets and exempted most U.S. allies
- Trump's approach proposed requiring foreign governments to provide security guarantees or data center investments for exports exceeding 200,000 AI chips

**Controversy:**
- Internal disagreement within Trump administration on balancing AI dominance and national security
- Commerce Dept called Biden's framework "burdensome, overly interventionist, and disastrous"
- However, insiders say the withdrawn draft was also complex and restrictive

**Status:** Commerce Department indicated on March 5 they're still working on new rules different from Biden's framework.
